Ultimate Tic-Tac-Toe Rules

Master the ultimate challenge with our step-by-step guide to Ultimate Tic-Tac-Toe. It's more than just a game — it's an adventure in strategic depth.

Board Overview

The game consists of a large 3×3 global board, each field of which contains a smaller 3×3 mini-board. The total number of game cells is 3×3×3×3 = 81.

Each mini-board is marked when conquered

Basic Rules

Rule 1 · First Move

The first move can be determined by rolling dice or flipping a coin, or players can agree verbally. In subsequent games, the right to start passes between players.

Rule 2 · Opening Move

The game begins with a player making a move in any of the 81 free cells.

Rule 3 · Sending the Opponent

Each move "sends" the opponent to the corresponding mini-board. For example, if X moves in the bottom-right cell of any mini-board, O must play in the bottom-right mini-board next.

Rule 4 · Turn Continuation

In response, O "sends" the opponent to a mini-board of their choosing, and turns continue in this fashion.

Rule 5 · Conquering a Mini-Board

A mini-board is conquered by the player who aligns three in a row in that specific mini-board.

Rule 6 · Draws

If a tie is reached on a mini-board, it is considered a draw and does not form part of any three-in-a-row on the larger board.

Rule 7 · Free Move

If a player is sent to a mini-board where a victory has already been claimed or a tie has occurred, they may choose any available space on the larger board.

Game Result

Win Condition

A player wins by lining up three conquered mini-boards in a row on the large board.

→
X wins a row → Victory
Draw

If neither player lines up three conquered mini-boards in a row, the game is a draw.
